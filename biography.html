Biography

Childhood Years

Carl's life began in Marikina City, Philippines, on July 26, 2005. Seven years later, his sister arrived in the family. His early years were spent with his parents until his father went abroad to provide for them. This was hard for Carl, but he understood his father's sacrifice. His relationship with his paternal grandparents is bittersweet, and living with them presented challenges, particularly with the tension between his mother and grandmother. Despite this, Carl's parents worked hard to make ends meet. His mother focused on raising their children while his father worked overseas. Before elementary school, he attended a small preparatory school where he excelled rapidly academically. He didn’t like the environment in his class before; not long after he graduated preparatory grade, Carl attended a Christian elementary school, where his extroverted personality was well-known by his peers. He was a spirited child with a strong faith in God; academics came easy for him, and he consistently earned excellent academic performance. However, this changed with this last and sixth academic year, when his grades began declining. His final year of elementary school was bittersweet, making it more complicated with his difficult classmates. With that, he enjoyed sports and music before, alongside video games. He’d rather play for hours than study for a test or quiz. He also enjoyed playing with his friends, riding bikes, and doing other activities without causing too much stress. He’d sometimes blast their home speakers in full volume with his favorite music disks playing. His academic decline was disappointing; however, Carl maintained determination and optimism about his future. He dreamed of becoming a chef, a doctor, or an engineer, although his path remained unclear. His teenage years brought about significant changes in his life. His younger years were a period of self-growth filled with new experiences and lessons.

Teenage Years

Carl attended APEC Schools Marikina Heights for high school in his teenage years from 2017 to 2023. Coming from a small elementary class, he experienced a significant culture shock back in seventh grade; suddenly surrounded by many students, he quickly adjusted to the environment and started to make early friends later on. He enjoyed the people at APEC and made friends with his teachers. He made great friends in his first year of high school but also lost others. His first two years were an academic struggle, and he did not find his momentum. But something clicked from ninth grade onward; he excelled academically, demonstrating his true academic potential. APEC became a place where he met new people and made exciting memories; some of his friends remain strong until today. His passion for music ignited in ninth grade when he picked up a bass guitar that he still plays enthusiastically. He also enjoyed videography, photography, and editing, which made him even more familiar with technology. Coding was his favorite pastime, and it made it clear what future would be ahead of him. Cycling became one of his favorite hobbies during his last senior year, a passion that sadly faded after a heartbreak. He made his mark during their fun week in his final year, performing guitar at an event and earning fame. Not everything in his high school was a happy memory; there were also sad memories. Aside from being a significant figure in APEC, he was known for his friendly actions and was always approachable and kind to those around him. His leadership skills were tested when he led his research group, winning an award for their prototype. Another significant event was his judging a science day in APEC, which he was proud of due to his project being highlighted by other groups. He discovered his strengths and passions in his high school years. The transition from a small elementary school made his life change. He learned to adapt to different environments: resilience, growth, and optimism.

College Years

For the present years, Carl has attended the University of the East, pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Information Technology. Ignited by his passion for computers, he is eager to learn more about programming, web development, and others. He is currently a second-year student with more spare time to deepen his knowledge. Carl wasn’t sure about information technology initially, as he wanted to pursue a career in law or medicine. That said, he only chose the degree due to his initial knowledge of technology. He was not initially interested in attending the University of the East, but his friend suggested doing so. His first year was filled with excitement and eagerness, and he kept working on new activities and other academics while working on his self-image. He quickly made new friends with his extroverted personality, but he is puzzled by his new groups to join with. He vowed to create memories and experiences. Being in the university, Carl slowly regained his potential for his passion for music, and he is currently the Vice President of the University of the East - Jam Sessions Manila. Aside from being a student, he also loves being involved in student organizations, but that is soon fading away for personal reasons. He is also skilled in other programming-related projects like Arduino and Android SDKs. He does not excel well in web development and back-end development. During his second year, he is experimenting with different projects, such as game development via the Monogame framework based on the obsolete XNA framework. With these academics in mind, Carl is a hard-working student who is always willing to learn. More experiences are yet to cross his headings as he embarks on a journey for success. However, Carl's journey is still something that he has to work and hold onto. He never gives up with the harshest moments of his life and always keeps on going even though everything is falling apart. He just has one motto in life. "All things come to an end".
